About Roxane Jessi

Roxane Jessi is a writer, adventurer, and global citizen who has spent her life exploring communities on the edges of the world. Having travelled to over 60 countries, she has a rare capacity to immerse herself in cultures radically different from her own — and to find the humanity at their core.

Her professional career has taken her to some of the world's most complex environments. She has worked on Congolese police reform, Iraq counterterrorism policy, and women's rights initiatives across sub-Saharan Africa. This experience of witnessing communities under pressure — broken, rebuilding, or forging something entirely new — shapes everything she writes.

"I've worked in places where communities have been torn apart. Burning Man showed me what it looks like when people choose to build something together from nothing."

In 2018, Roxane turned her attention to the global Burning Man community, setting out to attend seven Regional events across five continents. What she found — in the Israeli Negev, the Spanish Monegros, the South African Tankwa, the Australian Outback and the Nevada desert — was a movement with something urgent to say about how humans can live together.

She has now attended over 30 Burning Man events worldwide, including more than eight consecutive years at Black Rock City. She writes about these experiences with the eye of a journalist, the heart of a participant, and the perspective of someone who has seen what broken communities look like up close.

Roxane is based in Europe and continues to travel to Burning Man Regional events around the world.
