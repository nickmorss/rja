About the Book

Overview and Updates

The Book

What happens when a career humanitarian — someone who has worked on Congolese police reform, Iraq counterterrorism, and women's rights across Africa — turns her gaze toward the global Burning Man community?

Roxane Jessi set out to find the answer. In 2018 she embarked on a journey to attend seven Burning Man Regional events across five continents, immersing herself in the communities that are building something remarkable in the world's most inhospitable environments.

"It is a rare thing to simultaneously get the Burn you want and the Burn you need."

The Journey

From the Israeli Negev Desert at Midburn to the red earth of the Tankwa Karoo at Afrikaburn, from the eucalyptus forests of Western Australia at Blazing Swan to the ancient landscape of the Spanish Monegros at Nowhere — each event offered a window into a culture determined to reimagine the world from the ground up.

Along the way, Roxane encountered artists, activists, dreamers and misfits — people who have found in these temporary cities something the default world struggles to provide: radical community, unconditional participation, and a shared sense of purpose.

The Events

The seven Burning Man Regionals featured in the book span the globe and range from intimate gatherings of 250 to city-scale events of 80,000:

Burning Japan — 250 participants in the forests of Japan
Blazing Swan — 3,000 participants in the Australian Outback
Afrikaburn — 8,000 participants in South Africa's Tankwa desert
Midburn — 5,000 participants in Israel's Negev desert
Nowhere — 3,000 participants in Spain's Monegros desert
Fuego Austral — participants gathering in South America
Black Rock City — 80,000 participants in Nevada's Black Rock Desert
